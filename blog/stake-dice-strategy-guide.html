How Stake Dice Works

Stake Dice is beautifully simple:

  1. A random number between 0.00 and 100.00 is generated for each roll
  2. You choose a target number (the "roll over" or "roll under" point)
  3. You choose your bet amount
  4. The dice rolls. If your prediction is correct, you win
  5. Example: Set target at 50, bet on "under". If the roll is 1-49.99, you win. If it's 50-100, you lose.

The closer your target is to 0 or 100, the higher your potential payout — but the lower your chance of winning. It's a pure risk-reward tradeoff that you control.

Best Dice Strategies Compared

✅ Strategy 1: Conservative Play (Best for Beginners)

Target: Roll under 49 (49% win rate, ~2.00x payout)

Bet sizing: 1-2% of bankroll per roll

Why it works: You win almost every other roll, keeping your bankroll stable with low variance. Your balance goes up and down gradually rather than swinging wildly.

Best for: New players, bankroll building, low-risk entertainment

⚠️ Strategy 2: Moderate Risk (Balanced Approach)

Target: Roll under 24 (24% win rate, ~4.00x payout)

Bet sizing: 0.5-1% of bankroll per roll

Why it works: You win about 1 in 4 rolls but pay out 4x when you do. The balance between win frequency and payout size creates an exciting but manageable experience.

Best for: Players with a moderate bankroll who want more action

⚠️ Strategy 3: Martingale (Double After Loss)

Target: Roll under 49 (49% win rate)

Method: Start with a base bet. After every loss, double the bet. After a win, return to the base bet.

Why it works: When you eventually win, you recover all previous losses plus one base bet profit. In theory, you always profit eventually.

The catch: A long losing streak (7+ in a row) will blow through your bankroll. You need a large bankroll to survive the doubling. With a $100 bankroll and $1 base bet, 10 consecutive losses = $1,023 in losses you can't recover.

Best for: Players with large bankrolls (200+ base bets)

🔴 Strategy 4: High Roller (Not Recommended)

Target: Roll under 5 or lower (5% or less win rate, 20x+ payout)

Why it's risky: You'll lose 19 out of 20 rolls on average. To sustain this strategy, you need an enormous bankroll relative to your bet size. Even experienced players with large bankrolls can go broke quickly.

Only for: Players who fully understand variance and can afford to lose their entire session bankroll

Bankroll Management — The Most Important Skill

No strategy can overcome poor bankroll management. Here are the rules that separate smart players from those who go broke:

  • Never bet more than 2% of your bankroll per roll — If you have $100, your max bet should be $2. This gives you 50 rolls even if every single one loses.
  • Set a session limit — Decide before you start how much you're willing to lose. If you lose it, stop. Come back another day.
  • Set a win goal — Decide how much profit would make you happy. If you hit it, consider stopping or only playing with your winnings.
  • Never chase losses — If you're on a losing streak, don't increase your bets to "win it back." Stick to your bet sizing plan.
  • Separate your gambling funds — Never gamble with money you need for rent, bills, or other essentials. Only play with money you can afford to lose entirely.

Auto Bet Settings

Stake Dice has a powerful auto bet feature that lets you automate your strategy. Here's how to use it effectively:

  • Number of bets: Set a maximum (e.g., 100 or 500) so you don't accidentally run forever
  • Stop on profit: Set a profit target. When your net profit reaches this amount, auto bet stops.
  • Stop on loss: Set a loss limit. When your net loss reaches this amount, auto bet stops.
  • On win/loss: Choose whether to reset to base bet, increase by a percentage, or keep the same after each outcome
  • Recommended setup: Target 49% (2x payout), base bet = 1% of bankroll, stop on profit = +50% of bankroll, stop on loss = -20% of bankroll

Pro tip: Always set stop limits. Auto bet without a stop condition is the fastest way to lose your entire bankroll. Automated strategies without guardrails are dangerous.

FAQ — Stake Dice Strategy

Can you actually make money playing Stake Dice?+

In the long run, no. The 2% house edge means the casino has a mathematical advantage on every roll. Some players win in the short term due to variance, but over thousands of rolls, the house edge always wins. Play for entertainment, not as an income source.

Is Stake Dice rigged?+

No. Stake Dice is fully provably fair. You can verify every single roll using the cryptographic verification tool. The result is determined by the server seed, client seed, and nonce — and none of these can be changed after your bet.

What's the best target number for Stake Dice?+

For beginners, we recommend a 49% target (roll under 49) with 1-2% bankroll bets per roll. This gives you nearly 50/50 odds with manageable variance. Higher targets pay more but lose more often.

Does the Martingale strategy work on Stake Dice?+

In theory, yes — you always recover losses plus profit when you eventually win. In practice, long losing streaks happen more often than people expect. With a 49% win rate, a 10-loss streak happens roughly once every 500 sequences. You need a bankroll that can handle 7-8 doublings to use Martingale safely.

What's the minimum bet on Stake Dice?+

The minimum bet varies by cryptocurrency but is generally very low — often equivalent to $0.10 or less. This makes Stake Dice accessible for players with small bankrolls.

How do I verify that Stake Dice is fair?+

Go to your account settings, find "Provably Fair", and enter the server seed, client seed, and nonce from any roll. The tool will calculate the result and confirm it matches what you saw in the game. Alternatively, use third-party verification tools like StakeStats.
